From my perspective, serverless not only describes the instant availability of IT infrastructure for cost-effective application development. It's also a application-specific and reproducible configuration using a description library such as the AWS-CDK. Serverless is not just one of many Buzzwords in software development. It's a state of mind or a basic attitude. These mindset will change the current engineering techniques, architectures and best practices in the future. With serverless we can take care of business logic and its processes in the core business domain.

Serverless is not first and foremost about to create a complete IT- or Softwareapplication-Platform to use it in the future. It's about developing software that serves your business. You only pay for that in the company what you really need and not what you think to brew in the future.

In a serverless architecture you only need to think about the infrastructure scaling in special cases. You only pay more when you have more users, when your application reaches a large audience. It should always be like this!

Migrating existing applications to a serverless architecture is not easy. Usally you will spend more time at first to change your mindset as writing code or infrastructure. But neither is your core business anyway!

With serverless you can concentrate on your core domain and your business processes. Focus on that what you can do - your core business domain - and not what only concerns you. Application development within a serverless architecture and the appropriate state of mind, all stackholders of a project or product do that what they are really experts at!
